Ch. Shivaji Maharaj

1630-1680



About The Maharaj



The founder of the Maratha Kingdom, a natural leader and fighter Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was born on February 19, 1630, in the prestigious Shivneri Fort and was formally crowned as Chhatrapati of Raigad on June 6, 1674.

Shivaji Maharaj was a valiant king and a secular ruler who known for his bravery and tactics. He won numerous wars against the Mughals and established a competent and progressive civil rule with the help of a disciplined military and well-structured administrative organisations. He expanded his army from 2,000 soldiers to 100,000.

Shivaji Maharaj is also known as the Father of Indian Navy because he was the one, who first realised the importance of having a naval force and strategically established a navy and forts at the coastline to defend the Konkan side of Maharashtra. He was a faithful supporter of women and their honour. He opposed all kinds of violence, harassment and dishonour against women.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was widely known for his guerrilla warfare tactics also called as the 'Mountain Rat'.
